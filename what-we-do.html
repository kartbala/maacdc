What We Do

We set priorities, write the asks, keep score, and testify — turning lived experience into policy the District has to answer for.

Our north star

Principles for arterial street space

Adopted unanimously in June 2024, this hierarchy tells the District how to allocate the limited space on its busiest streets — highest priority first.

  1. Wide, smooth, connected sidewalksThe foundation. Without them, nothing else is reachable.
  2. Protected, 24/7 bus lanesReliable transit that moves people, not parked cars.
  3. ADA pick-up / drop-off zonesSafe, dedicated curb space for accessible vehicles.
  4. Non-ADA private car storageThe lowest priority — and one the District should never subsidize.

Where we push

Key advocacy areas

Daylighting & sightlines

DC law requires clear sightlines at intersections. With roughly 7,000 intersections never checked, we press DDOT to treat daylighting as the legal duty it is — not “one of many treatments.”

ADA pick-up / drop-off zones

We’ve called for a 25-zone downtown PUDO pilot so wheelchair users and paratransit riders aren’t forced to load and unload in moving traffic.

Bus-stop visibility

A 50-stop pilot of red pavement markings at bus stops — keeping the stop clear so buses can pull to the curb and riders can board safely.

Horizontal wayfinding

Tactile, underfoot guidance at bus stops to solve the “last ten feet” — the gap between the sidewalk and the bus door that blind riders cross blind.

Wheelchair-accessible vehicles

We demand mandatory response times, real-time tracking, and side-loading WAV designs — and that at least half of future autonomous-vehicle fleets be wheelchair accessible.

Vision Zero accountability

With 52+ deaths and 6,600+ injuries on DC roads in 2024, we hold the District to the safety promises it has made — and document where it has fallen short.

Recent letters & resolutions

    ADA Pick-Up / Drop-Off (PUDO) Zones

    Calling for a 25-zone downtown pilot within 90 days. Being carried to WMATA’s MetroAccess Subcommittee.

  • Letter · March 2026

    Bus-Stop Red Pavement Markings

    A 50-stop pilot using durable marking material to keep bus stops clear. Presented to WMATA’s Bus & Rail Subcommittee.

  • Letter · March 2026

    Horizontal Wayfinding Pilot

    A 3–5 stop pilot, under $50K, to test underfoot tactile guidance at bus stops.

  • Questions · November 2025

    IPMA Road-Diet Methodology

    Detailed technical questions to DDOT challenging how road-diet feasibility is judged — from person-vs-vehicle throughput to ward-level transit-time disparities.

    Arterial Street Principles & Daylighting

    Unanimous resolutions establishing the street-space priority hierarchy and demanding enforcement of DC sightline laws.
