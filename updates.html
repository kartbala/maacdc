Letters & recommendations

Updates & recommendations

A public record of MAAC’s advocacy — from recent letters to the DC Council back to the Council’s early work in 2021.

  • Letter to DC Council · April 27, 2026

    Accessibility Requirements in AV Legislation

    MAAC wrote to all DC Councilmembers urging that any legislation permitting the testing or operation of automated vehicle (AV) passenger service include accessibility requirements for disabled customers — wheelchair-accessible vehicles, deployment in underserved areas, pricing transparency and protections, and stakeholder engagement with disability and advocacy groups.

    Read the letter to DC Council (PDF) →

  • Council testimony · February 18, 2026

    Daylighting & Bus Priority

    Testimony before the DC Council Committee on Transportation & the Environment, pressing DDOT on daylighting — keeping intersections and crosswalks clear — and on bus priority. The testimony documents the agency’s response to MAAC’s daylighting request and the institutional barriers to safer streets.

    Read the testimony (PDF) →

  • Letter to DDOT · February 14, 2026

    ADA Pick-Up / Drop-Off Zones

    A letter to DDOT Director Kershbaum on the District’s shortage of ADA pick-up/drop-off (PUDO) zones, how daylighting and curbside revenue pressures have squeezed accessible loading out of the curb, and what it would take to restore it.

    Read the letter to DDOT (PDF) →

  • Letter to DDOT · February 14, 2026

    Red Pavement at Bus Stops

    A letter to DDOT Director Kershbaum recommending durable red pavement at bus zones so buses can pull flush to the curb for wheelchair boarding — with the regulatory basis (MUTCD 11th Edition), results from other cities, and phased cost estimates.

    Read the letter to DDOT (PDF) →

  • Letter to DDOT · February 14, 2026

    Horizontal Wayfinding Pilot

    A letter to DDOT proposing a 3-to-5-stop pilot of sidewalk wayfinding markings to help transit riders — including people with low vision — navigate to stops, with a path to classify them as traffic-control devices via an FHWA Request to Experiment.

    Read the letter to DDOT (PDF) →

  • Letter to DFHV · June 19, 2025

    Wheelchair-Accessible Vehicle Service Follow-Up

    A follow-up letter to the Department of For-Hire Vehicles (DFHV) Director Rogers requesting concrete data on wheelchair-accessible vehicle (WAV) service — fulfillment rates, wait times, and evening/overnight availability — following MAAC’s earlier recommendations.

    Read the letter to DFHV (PDF) →

  • Recommendation · October 18, 2021

    Georgetown Sidewalk Extension Recommendation

    At a public meeting on October 13, 2021, MAAC recommended to DC Councilmember Mary Cheh and DDOT Director Everett Lott that the District prohibit scooters and bikes from riding on the sidewalks in Georgetown’s commercial business district.

    Read the Georgetown Site Visit Report →

  • Update · March 10, 2021

    moveDC

    moveDC is the District’s long-range transportation plan. Under the Mayor’s direction, it sets the goals, policies, strategies, and metrics DDOT uses to address transportation needs across all eight wards. MAAC encouraged residents to weigh in during the 2021 public comment period.

    Learn about moveDC →

  • Update · March 10, 2021

    Vision Zero

    Through DC’s Vision Zero initiative, the District set a goal of reaching zero traffic fatalities and serious injuries — pursued through data, education, enforcement, and engineering. MAAC tracks the District’s progress against that promise.

    DDOT Vision Zero →
